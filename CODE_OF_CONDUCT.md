# Code of Conduct

Pushboy is an open-source project for building notification infrastructure. The project should be useful, direct, and respectful for maintainers and contributors.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Assume good intent, but accept correction when impact and intent differ.
- Keep technical disagreement focused on evidence, behavior, tradeoffs, and maintainability.
- Respect maintainer time by keeping issues and pull requests clear, scoped, and reproducible.
- Avoid sharing private user data, production credentials, device tokens, customer payloads, or confidential logs.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or personal attacks.
- Discrimination or exclusionary language.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions.
- Reporting vulnerabilities publicly instead of following [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
- Spamming issues, discussions, pull requests, or maintainer contact channels.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments, issues, pull requests, discussions, or other contributions that violate this code of conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict participation for repeated or severe violations.

## Reporting

Report conduct issues to the maintainers through a private channel. If the report involves a security vulnerability, follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) instead.

Reports should include:

- What happened.
- Where it happened.
- Who was involved.
- Any relevant links or screenshots.
- Whether immediate action is needed.

Maintainers will review reports in good faith and handle them with appropriate privacy.
